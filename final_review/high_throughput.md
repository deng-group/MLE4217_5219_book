# High-Throughput Simulation

## What we covered

- The logic of turning single calculations into scalable workflows for screening and discovery.
- The connection between automation, databases, and many-structure simulation campaigns.
- Thermodynamic ideas used in high-throughput materials evaluation, including stability and convex hulls.
- Practical workflow tools and codes used to organize calculations and extract useful trends.

## What you should be able to do

- Explain why workflow automation is important in modern materials research.
- Describe the main steps in a high-throughput pipeline from structure generation to stored results.
- Explain the meaning of formation energy, phase stability, and convex hull position at a practical level.
- Interpret why a phase above the hull is unstable and what decomposition tendency means.
- Connect high-throughput simulation with later tasks such as data mining, screening, and model training.
