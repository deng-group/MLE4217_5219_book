# Atomistic Models: Models and Theories I

## What we covered

- The role of atomistic modelling within a broader multiscale picture.
- Force fields as approximate energy models built from chosen functional forms and parameters.
- The balance between speed, interpretability, fitting scope, and transferability in classical simulations.
- Practical modelling workflows using ASE and related atomistic tools.

## What you should be able to do

- Explain where force-field methods fit within computational materials science.
- Compare a classical force field with a more general electronic-structure method at a high level.
- Discuss why a model can perform well in one domain and poorly outside the data or chemistry it was designed for.
- Describe a simple ASE-based workflow for building, relaxing, and analyzing atomistic structures.
