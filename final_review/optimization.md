# Optimization

## What we covered

- Energy landscapes, local minima, and the difference between finding a nearby minimum and finding the best one globally.
- Local optimization methods such as gradient descent, conjugate gradient, and Newton-style approaches.
- Global search ideas including stochastic exploration and structure-search strategies.
- Practical considerations when choosing an optimizer for a given problem.

## What you should be able to do

- Explain why different local optimizers can converge to the same minimum with different efficiency.
- Describe what information is used by gradient descent, conjugate gradient, and Newton-based methods.
- Explain why tighter convergence does not solve the problem of being trapped in a local basin.
- Describe why stochastic global methods can give different outcomes across runs.
- Discuss how to choose a method based on cost, reliability, and the roughness of the landscape.
