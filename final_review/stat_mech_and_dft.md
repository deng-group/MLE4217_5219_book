# Statistical Mechanics and Electronic Structure: Models and Theories II

## What we covered

- Statistical-mechanics ideas behind finite-temperature simulation and sampling.
- Molecular dynamics, Monte Carlo, and kinetic Monte Carlo as different ways to study atomistic behavior.
- Quantum-mechanical and electronic-structure foundations behind DFT calculations.
- Practical strengths and limits of different modelling choices for structure, energy, and temperature-dependent questions.

## What you should be able to do

- Explain the difference between MD, MC, and KMC in terms of what is being sampled or evolved.
- Describe why equilibration matters before averaging quantities from a trajectory.
- Explain why time step choice affects both stability and physical accuracy in MD.
- State why the thermodynamic ensemble matters when extracting quantities such as thermal expansion.
- Summarize what DFT aims to compute and why approximations such as functionals and pseudopotentials are used.
- Compare when an atomistic question is better matched to classical sampling, DFT, or a different workflow.
